Critiques of Geodemographics
April 23, 2023 by Gordon McLachlan

Some authors focus on statistical concerns; geodemographics are usually created out with the traditional statistical frame work (Harris et al., 2007). Voas & Williamson (2001) proposed that variances between individual geodemographic classifications hide the potential wide diversity found within the classifications. If this was the case, then the differences found in geodemographics could be a result of the clustering method rather than actual socio-economic variances (Harris et al., 2007). While it is common that a standardised value indicating the prevalence or strength of a variable is provided, the measure of variance within the classification or a statistical significance between classifications are not (Harris et al., 2007). This can make it difficult to determine the significance of apparent trends found during analysis (Harris et al., 2007).
