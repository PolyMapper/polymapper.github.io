Agent Based Modelling
October 7, 2022 by Gordon McLachlan

At a basic level MSMs are typically large synthetic populations contained within databases, with each individual holding potentially many variables or attributes. The behaviours and states of these individual units are dictated by predefined rules applied in isolation. As a consequence, individuals do not directly interact with each other. ABMs, on the other hand, are built from idea of interacting autonomous individuals, with each single agent having its own set of rules that dictate behaviour which can affect the manner of interaction between agents and the environment.
