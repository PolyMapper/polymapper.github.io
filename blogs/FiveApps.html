5 Everyday Applications of GIS You Didn’t Know About

November 21, 2024 by Gordon McLachlan

When someone mentions GIS (Geographical Information Systems), it’s easy to picture maps, satellite imagery, or tools used in academic or government projects. But GIS is far more integrated into everyday life than most of us realise. From smoothing out your morning commute to ensuring fresh groceries arrive at your local store, GIS quietly plays a part in making our routines easier and more efficient.

Here are five surprising ways GIS is at work behind the scenes:

1. Making Your Deliveries Faster

Ever wondered how your online orders show up at your doorstep so quickly? Logistics companies use GIS to optimise delivery routes, factoring in real-time traffic, road closures, and weather conditions. By analysing this data, GIS helps drivers take the fastest and most fuel-efficient paths, cutting down costs and delivery times.

Take UPS or Amazon, for example. Their advanced GIS tools save millions of miles annually by fine-tuning delivery routes. It’s not just good for your next-day delivery; it’s also a win for the environment, reducing emissions on a massive scale.

2. Protecting Wildlife and Ecosystems

Out in the wild, GIS is a lifeline for conservation efforts. Scientists and conservationists rely on GIS to monitor animal movements and map critical habitats. These insights help protect endangered species and maintain ecosystem balance.

For instance, GIS is used to track elephants as they roam the African savannah, allowing researchers to pinpoint poaching hotspots. Similarly, in marine biology, GIS helps track whale migrations, guiding shipping companies to reroute vessels and avoid collisions with these majestic creatures.

3. Helping During Disasters

When a natural disaster strikes, quick decisions save lives. GIS is the tool emergency responders rely on to understand what’s happening in real time. By mapping flood zones, wildfire progress, or hurricane impacts, GIS helps teams prioritise rescue efforts and allocate resources efficiently.

During California’s wildfire seasons, for example, GIS maps show the spread of fires and highlight safe evacuation routes. These tools help firefighters and first responders coordinate efforts, minimising damage and saving lives.

4. Improving Public Transport

If your bus route seems to have improved lately, you might have GIS to thank. Transportation planners use GIS to study traffic patterns, track buses and trains, and adjust routes based on population shifts or congestion.

Cities like London and New York rely on GIS to monitor passenger numbers and reduce delays. By analysing data in real time, they can reroute buses to avoid gridlocked streets or introduce new stops in underserved areas. The result? Public transport that runs more smoothly for everyone.

5. Shaping Your Holiday Plans

When you’re browsing holiday destinations online, GIS is often working behind the scenes. Travel platforms use GIS to map out hotels, attractions, and restaurants, giving you an easy way to explore your options.

For instance, TripAdvisor and Airbnb use GIS to show detailed maps of cities, highlighting how close accommodations are to landmarks, beaches, or public transport. It’s a practical tool that helps travellers make informed decisions and maximise their trips.

The Everyday Power of GIS

GIS may seem like a technical tool reserved for experts, but it’s deeply embedded in our daily lives. It helps us track wildlife, respond to emergencies, improve services, and even plan our holidays. Every time your package arrives earlier than expected, your train avoids a delay, or you discover a hidden gem on holiday, GIS has likely played a role.

So, the next time life feels a little more seamless, take a moment to appreciate the invisible hand of GIS working quietly in the background.
